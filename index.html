His Story

Born on January 5th 1941, Miyazaki grew up during WWII where he had to run away from bombings. These events may have influence his future works. Miyazaki was known for being a perfectionist, and he worked as an animator during his early days. It was not until he found Studio Ghibi that his films started to gain international attention. What makes his film interesting is that he incorporates realistic themes into his films. Some of these themes can be seen to be influenced by his past, such as his stance on anti-war. As a perfectionist, Miyazaki wanted to be proud of every single work, since they were a reflection of himself. As a result, he was often strict and demanding on his staff. "We are born with infinite possibilities, only to give up on one after another" Miyazaki believed that making decisions means giving something up, and it is inevitable. Miyazaki has retired multiple times, but came back to the industry because he wants to keep fighting and creating until he dies.
